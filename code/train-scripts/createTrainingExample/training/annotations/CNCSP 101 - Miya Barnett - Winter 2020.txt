course materials there is no required textbook for this course. all required readings are available through gauchospace (http://gauchospace.ucsb.edu). materials on gauchospace are made available exclusively for use by students enrolled in this course and must not be distributed to people who are not enrolled in the course. please purchase an iclicker (either iclicker1 or iclicker2) and bring it to each lecture. suggested references (available in actual or virtual bookstores): american psychological association. (2009). publication manual of the american psychological association (6th ed.). washington, dc: author. strunk, w., & white, e. b. (any year). elements of style (any edition). longman. course description this course will introduce students to theory and practice of psychotherapy and other helping relationships. students will learn about counseling, assessment, ethics, and helping skills. students will gain experience by serving as practice counseling clients or by volunteering in a relevant service setting. by the end of this course, students will be able to 1. discuss the theoretical and empirical support for helping skills, relationships, and psychotherapy approaches 2. describe the characteristics, experiences, biases, identities, and motivations that may affect their helping relationships 3. conceptualize help-seekers and their problems 4. demonstrate and reflect on use of helping skills 5. apply professional ethics to helping scenarios 6. discuss factors that facilitate and limit behavioral change 7. describe ways in which helping skills are used in various settings>>5
