_____________________________________________________________________________ university of california, santa barbara global studies 124 - soc 138g * spring, 2020 * prof. juergensmeyer ______________________________________________________________________________ global conflict ______________________________________________________________________________ focus: since the beginning of this century, these two decades have been full of conflict terrorism, new nationalisms, and crises that are economic, environmental, and health related. the goal of this class is to explore policy analyses and conflict resolution approaches with reference to real life situations. this means that we will explore case studies of conflicts, primarily in the middle east. lectures, guest speakers, and readings will set the contexts and relate the cases to contemporary global issues. since this course will focus on current events, topics may shift and the schedule of speakers may change with very little notice. on-line format: due to the covid-19 pandemic, the class will be on line. information will posted on gauchospace, so check it regularly. lectures will be given on zoom. the topics for the two in-class writing assignments and the questions for the final multiple choice exam will be posted on gauchospace at the beginning of the class times scheduled; students will have an hour and a half to complete each assignment and test, and upload their paper/test answers onto gauchospace. student projects: there are two in-class writing projects. the first is a one-page policy brief that will provide clarification and policy options related to a case discussed in class. the other will be a brief conflict resolution that will take a dispute discussed in class and argue both sides, and then present a resolution using the principles of conflict resolution. the multiple choice exam will cover information provided in lectures, case studies, the selections in the reader, and web-site readings. as mentioned above, i will post the topics for the written assignment and questions for the test on gauchospace at the beginning of the class time and you will post your answers on gauchospace within an hour and half after getting the topic or questions. readings: readings for the course will be found in the global conflict reader prepared for the class on sale at the sbprinter copy center in the ucen; both digital and paper versions are availablethe paper version can be sent to your home or wherever you are. also required are two books: terror in the mind of god: the global rise of religious violence- fourth edition (university of california press, 2017) and gandhis way: a handbook of conflict resolution  revised edition (university of california press, 2005), both by juergensmeyer. make sure you have the 4th edition of terror and the revised edition of gandhis way. additional readings for the cases will be posted on the class web site located at www.gauchospace.ucsb.edu. students are also expected to have a news source on their computer browser or app, such as cnn (www.cnn.com/world), la times (www.latimes.com),ny times (www.nytimes.com),national public radio (www.npr.org), al jazeera (www.aljazeera.com), economist (www.economist.com)in order to be knowledgeable about world affairs. email for prof juergensmeyer (juergens@global.ucsb.edu) tas: sam salour (soc);
