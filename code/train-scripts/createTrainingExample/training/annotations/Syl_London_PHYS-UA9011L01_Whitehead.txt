tba  prerequisites  math-ua 121 calculus i or equivalent course overview and goals this course begins a two semester introduction to physics (lecture, lab sessions and recitation) intended primarily for pre-professional students and for those majoring in a science other than physics. class time is comprised of 2/5 hours per week of lectures, a 1 hour recitation and a 2 hour laboratory session each week. upon completion of this course, students will be able to:  students will learn about kinematics and dynamics of particles; force, momentum, work, energy; gravitation; circular, angular and harmonic motion; waves; mechanical and thermal properties of solids, liquids and gases.  students will develop critical thinking and problem solving skills  students will develop analytical and objective report writing in the laboratory. course requirements grading of assignments the grade for this course will be determined according to these assessment components: assignments/ activities description of assignment % of final grade due homework assignments 8 questions per week will be assigned 20% one week after class unless>>5
